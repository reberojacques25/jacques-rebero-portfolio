Project Context

Inyange Industries observed a noticeable decline in juice sales between 2023 and 2024, despite stable pricing and consistent production capacity. Management required a data-driven investigation to understand product-level and branch-level performance issues.

Objectives

  • Analyze sales trends across time and branches
  • Identify underperforming juice flavors
  • Evaluate the impact of stockouts on revenue
  • Provide actionable business recommendations

Data Used

  • Daily and weekly sales data by branch
  • Sales by juice flavor
  • Stockout frequency per branch
  • Revenue contribution per product

Tools & Methods

  • Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)
  • Microsoft Excel
  • Trend analysis and comparative analysis
  • Heatmaps and time-series visualization

Key Insights

  • Overall sales declined significantly in Q2 and Q3 of 2024
  • Mango and Pineapple flavors consistently underperformed
  • Nyabugogo and Kimironko branches showed frequent stockouts
  • Weekend sales outperformed weekday sales across all regions

Recommendations

  • Strengthen inventory forecasting in high-risk branches
  • Reposition or reformulate low-performing flavors
  • Introduce weekend promotions and bundled offers
  • Apply demand forecasting models for production planning
